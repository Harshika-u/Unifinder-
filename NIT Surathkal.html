Basic Information

The National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT-Calicut or NIT-C) is a public technical university and an institute of national importance governed by the NIT Act passed by the Parliament of India. The campus is situated 22 kilometres (14 mi) northeast of Kozhikode, on the Kozhikode–Mukkam Road. It was established in 1961 and was known as Calicut Regional Engineering College (CREC) until 2002. It is one of the National Institutes of Technology campuses established by the Government of India to impart high standard technical education to students from all over the country. NIT Calicut hosts a supercomputer on its campus, and has a dedicated nanotechnology department. NIT Calicut is ranked as one of the prestigious engineering institutions in India.

History

NNIT Rourkela began life as Regional Engineering College (REC), Rourkela, inaugurated on Independence Day, 1961, emphasizing India’s drive for technical self-sufficiency. The institution was granted full autonomy and rechristened as NIT Rourkela in 2002, gaining the prestigious status of Institute of National Importance by the NIT Act. The school has expanded from humble beginnings—one building housing everything—to a 700-acre campus supporting over 7,700 students, 390+ faculty, and a robust research culture. Recognized as a premier institute for science, technology, and research, NITR continues to punch above its weight in national and international rankings.

🏕️ Campus Life

NIT Rourkela’s campus is a self-contained universe—a sprawling 700-acre city on its own. Located on the eastern fringe of Rourkela, it houses over 7,700 students, 390+ faculty, administrative staff, and their families, fostering a unique academic and social community. Infrastructure is a major asset: the campus includes well-designed academic blocks, fully networked classrooms, modern laboratories, and state-of-the-art research centers. The central library, with over 60,000 volumes and 1,400 periodicals, doubles as a digital repository—containing every dissertation and thesis produced. This digital openness empowers both current students and global researchers. Hostel life is central, with nine halls of residence (seven for boys, two for girls), supporting over 3,000 boarders. Each hostel has badminton and volleyball courts, with further access to a sprawling sports complex offering cricket, football, basketball, tennis, swimming, and even a dedicated hockey stadium. Fitness enthusiasts frequent the gym; clubs and activity groups cater to every interest—technical, cultural, social, and entrepreneurial. Dining options are abundant, ranging from hostel messes serving North and South Indian meals to on-campus cafés and eateries. Convenience stores, health and recreation centers, a guest house, banks, ATMs, post office, and Wi-Fi connectivity all add to the campus’s self-sufficient feel. The academic calendar is balanced by a rich festive tradition: “Innovision” (the technical fest) and “Cosmopolitan Fest” (the cultural celebration) draw visitors nationally, offering avenues for talent showcase, collaboration, and celebration. Classroom schedules, generally between 8–11 a.m. and 1:15–4:15 p.m., leave afternoons and evenings for extracurricular pursuits. The flexible timetable, ample clubs, and vibrant peer group mean that academic challenges are offset by opportunities for growth in arts, sports, social work, and innovation. A plural and welcoming ethos is central to campus life at NITR. Students from every Indian region and abroad bring together a cosmopolitan mix. The pace is relentless, but the support systems—formal and informal—are strong, ensuring students balance academics with personal development. The emphasis is not just on acquiring knowledge, but also on learning time management, resilience, and teamwork, turning out graduates who are skilled and adaptable.

💼 Placements

Placement Highlights (2024-2025)

NIT Rourkela is renowned for its robust placements, consistently ranked among the top NITs in placement statistics and recruiter diversity. The Training and Placement Cell works continuously to align students with global and Indian corporate leaders, government organizations, and research institutions. Major recruiters include Microsoft, Goldman Sachs, Amazon, Samsung, Tata Steel, L&T, Deloitte, Flipkart, Capgemini, Infosys, Accenture, Oil India, Maruti Suzuki, and Hindalco, among many more. The 2025 statistics show around 95% placement of eligible B.Tech students, with the highest domestic package exceeding ₹46 lakh per annum. The average salary is reported between ₹14-17 lakh per annum for the most sought-after branches. Internship opportunities are equally prized, with students acquiring summer offers from marquee firms across IT, consulting, core engineering, analytics, and research. Placements are organized, with students prepared through resume workshops, soft skills training, technical skill ups, and mock interview sessions. Notably, the alumni network is leveraged extensively for referrals, mentorship, and real-world insight. Multinational corporation internships often turn into pre-placement offers, improving final stage conversion rates. A strong research and technical focus means many students also pursue higher studies at premier global institutions. The T&P Cell actively supports GRE/GMAT/CAT candidates, and hosts higher education workshops and counseling sessions. Alumni in academia serve as a bridge for aspiring research graduates. The placement landscape reflects NIT Rourkela’s industry connections and the adaptability of its students. Besides conventional placements, support exists for students opting for civil services, entrepreneurship, or core research. Placement rates, salary statistics, and recruiter quality continue to improve, maintaining the institute’s reputation as a launchpad for outstanding careers.
