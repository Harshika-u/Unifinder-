Basic Information

Banaras Hindu University (BHU) is a collegiate, central, and research university located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh. Established in 1916 by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, BHU is one of the largest residential universities in Asia with over 30,000 students. The university's main campus spans over 1,300 acres and was built on land donated by the Kashi Naresh. BHU is recognized as an Institute of Eminence and has been accredited with A++ grade by NAAC.

History

The foundation of BHU was laid in 1916 by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya with the cooperation of great personalities like Dr Annie Besant, who viewed it as the University of India. BHU was created under the Parliamentary legislation - B.H.U. Act 1915. The university played a significant role in the Indian independence movement. Over the years, BHU has grown into a multidisciplinary university with 6 institutes, 14 faculties, 140 departments, and 4 advanced centers.

RESEARCH PROGRAMS

BHU is renowned for its research programs across various disciplines. The university offers PhD programs in all departments, providing students with the opportunity to work on cutting-edge research projects under the guidance of experienced faculty members.

PhD Programs:

The PhD program at BHU is designed to train students to become independent researchers who can contribute to the advancement of knowledge in their chosen fields. The program involves coursework, comprehensive examinations, and original research leading to a thesis.

Research Areas:

  • Engineering: AI, Robotics, Renewable Energy, Nanotechnology
  • Medicine: Cancer Research, Neurology, Cardiology, Public Health
  • Sciences: Material Science, Environmental Science, Biotechnology
  • Agriculture: Crop Improvement, Soil Science, Agricultural Economics
  • Social Sciences: Indian Culture, Ancient History, Economics

Admission to the PhD program is through BHU's entrance test followed by interviews. The university provides fellowships and research assistantships to support PhD students during their studies.

üèï Campus Life

Banaras Hindu University has one of the most beautiful and spacious campuses in India, spread over 1,300 acres. The campus is known for its architectural grandeur, with the main building designed in Indo-Gothic style. The university has well-maintained gardens, wide roads, and a serene environment conducive to learning and research.

Hostels and Accommodation

BHU has a well-established hostel system with over 60 hostels that can accommodate more than 15,000 students. The hostels are equipped with modern amenities including Wi-Fi, mess facilities, common rooms, and sports facilities. Separate hostels are available for boys and girls, with some hostels specifically reserved for international students.

Sports and Recreation

BHU has excellent sports facilities including a stadium, gymnasium, swimming pool, tennis courts, basketball courts, cricket ground, and football ground. The university encourages students to participate in sports and has produced many national and international level athletes. The annual sports meet is a major event on campus.

Cultural Activities

Cultural life at BHU is vibrant and diverse. The university has several student clubs and societies that organize various cultural events throughout the year. The annual cultural festival, "Spandan," is one of the most anticipated events on campus, featuring music, dance, drama, art, and literature. The festival attracts participants from colleges across the country.

Facilities and Amenities

BHU provides state-of-the-art facilities to support academic and research activities. The campus has well-equipped laboratories, advanced research centers, a central library with over 1.3 million volumes, and modern classrooms. The university also has a health center, banking facilities, post office, shopping complex, and several cafeterias that cater to the diverse culinary preferences of the campus community.
