Basic Information

The Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS) is a premier institution for social work education in India. Established in 1936 as the Sir Dorabji Tata Graduate School of Social Work, it was renamed to its current name in 1944. In 1964, the University Grants Commission declared TISS as a deemed university under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956. The institute has been consistently ranked among the top universities in India for social work and social sciences.

History

The Tata Institute of Social Sciences was established in 1936 as the Sir Dorabji Tata Graduate School of Social Work, the first school of social work in India. It was founded with the objective of creating a professional cadre of social workers to address the social challenges of the time. The institute was renamed to Tata Institute of Social Sciences in 1944. In 1964, the University Grants Commission declared TISS as a deemed university under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956, enabling it to frame its own courses and award degrees. Over the years, TISS has expanded its academic programs and research activities to address emerging social issues and challenges. The institute has established multiple campuses across India, including in Mumbai (Main Campus), Tuljapur, Hyderabad, and Guwahati, to extend its reach and impact. TISS has also been a pioneer in introducing innovative academic programs and research initiatives in social work and social sciences.

Vision and Mission

  • To be an institution of excellence in higher education that continuously responds to changing social realities through the development and application of knowledge, towards creating a people-centered and ecologically sustainable society that promotes and protects dignity, equality, social justice and human rights for all.
  • To work towards just and equitable society by organizing teaching, research and extension programs in the field of social work and social sciences.
  • To promote the use of social work knowledge in addressing social problems and issues.
  • To collaborate with national and international organizations for the promotion of social work education and practice.
  • To develop human resources for social work practice, social work education and social development.

📝 Admissions – TISS 2025

1. Check Eligibility Criteria

  • Bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university
  • Minimum 50% aggregate marks (45% for SC/ST/OBC-NCL/PwD)
  • Final year students can also apply
  • Age limit: No upper age limit for most programs
1

2. Fill TISS Application Form

  • Visit appln.tiss.edu
  • Register with valid email ID and mobile number
  • Fill personal, academic, and program details
  • Upload required documents (photograph, signature, etc.)
  • Pay application fee (INR 1030 for General, INR 260 for SC/ST/OBC-NCL/PwD)
2

3. Prepare for TISSNET

  • TISS National Entrance Test (TISSNET) is a computer-based exam
  • Syllabus: English Proficiency, Mathematical Skills, General Awareness
  • Exam duration: 100 minutes
  • Total questions: 100 (MCQ type)
  • No negative marking
3

4. Appear for TISSNET

  • Exam usually conducted in January
  • Admit card available 1-2 weeks before the exam
  • Report to exam center on time with admit card and photo ID
  • Attempt all questions as there is no negative marking
4

5. Check TISSNET Results

  • Results usually announced in February
  • Check TISS website for results
  • Shortlisted candidates called for Pre-Interview Test and Personal Interview
  • Merit list prepared based on TISSNET, PIT, and PI scores
5

6. Attend Pre-Interview Test & Personal Interview

  • Pre-Interview Test (PIT) assesses subject knowledge and writing skills
  • Personal Interview (PI) evaluates aptitude, motivation, and suitability
  • Carry all required documents for verification
  • Dress formally and be prepared to discuss your interests and goals
6

7. Final Selection & Admission

  • Final merit list released based on combined scores
  • Selected candidates receive offer letters
  • Complete admission formalities by paying fees
  • Submit original documents for verification
  • Report to campus on the specified date
7

🏕️ Campus Life

Life at TISS is a unique blend of academic rigor and vibrant extracurricular activities. The institute provides a conducive environment for students to explore their interests, develop their skills, and engage in meaningful social action. With campuses in Mumbai, Tuljapur, Hyderabad, and Guwahati, TISS offers diverse experiences to its students.

Campuses

TISS has four campuses across India, each with its unique character and focus:

  • Mumbai Campus: The main campus located in Deonar, Mumbai. It houses most of the postgraduate programs and research centers. The campus has excellent facilities including libraries, computer labs, seminar halls, and sports facilities.
  • Tuljapur Campus: Located in Osmanabad district of Maharashtra, this campus focuses on rural development and social justice. It offers programs in social sciences with a specialization in rural development.
  • Hyderabad Campus: Established in 2012, this campus offers programs in social sciences, education, and human resources management. The campus has modern infrastructure and a vibrant student community.
  • Guwahati Campus: The newest campus located in Assam, it focuses on addressing the social issues of the northeastern region. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in social work and social sciences.

Hostel Life

TISS provides hostel facilities to students on all its campuses. The hostels are designed to create a home away from home for students, fostering a sense of community and belonging. Each hostel has a warden and resident mentors who guide and support students.

  • Separate hostels for male and female students
  • Well-furnished rooms with basic amenities
  • Common rooms with TV, newspapers, and recreational facilities
  • Wi-Fi connectivity throughout the campus
  • 24/7 security and medical facilities
  • Mess facilities providing nutritious and hygienic food

Student Activities

TISS has a vibrant student community with numerous clubs, committees, and groups that cater to diverse interests. These student-led initiatives provide opportunities for leadership, creativity, and social engagement.

  • Cultural Committee: Organizes cultural events, festivals, and performances throughout the year.
  • Sports Committee: Promotes sports and fitness activities, including inter-campus tournaments.
  • Debate and Literary Society: Organizes debates, elocution competitions, and literary events.
  • Film Club: Screens films and organizes discussions on social issues.
  • Volunteer Action: Engages students in community service and social action projects.
  • Gender and Sexuality Forum: Promotes discussions on gender issues and creates awareness about LGBTQ+ rights.

Annual Festival: Mélange

Mélange is the annual cultural festival of TISS, celebrating diversity, creativity, and social consciousness. It is a platform for students to showcase their talents, engage in intellectual discussions, and celebrate the spirit of social work. The festival includes:

  • Cultural performances by students and professional artists
  • Competitions in music, dance, drama, and fine arts
  • Lectures and panel discussions by eminent personalities
  • Film screenings and exhibitions
  • Sports events and tournaments
  • Food stalls representing different cuisines
