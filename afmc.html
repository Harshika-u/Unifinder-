Basic Information

The Armed Forces Medical College (AFMC) is a premier medical college in India, located in Pune. It was established in May 1948 after World War II on the recommendation of the BC Roy Committee by the amalgamation of various Army medical training centers. The college is managed by the Indian Armed Forces. It is affiliated to the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Nashik and is recognized by the Medical Council of India. Students of AFMC are known as "AFMCites". The college is fully residential with separate hostels for boys and girls. Residential accommodation is provided to all undergraduate students and most postgraduate students. The college also provides housing to its faculty and staff.

History

The Armed Forces Medical College was established on 1 May 1948. The college was set up after World War II on the recommendation of the BC Roy Committee by the amalgamation of various Army medical training centers. The college's first commandant was Colonel BM Gupta. The graduate wing was started on 4 August 1962. The college celebrated its golden jubilee in 1998 and diamond jubilee in 2008. AFMC is the only medical college in India where all facilities are provided free to students, including accommodation, books, messing, uniforms, and a monthly stipend. The college has consistently been ranked among the top medical colleges in India by various surveys and rankings.

Objectives of AFMC

  • To provide medical and paramedical education to the selected candidates for service in the Armed Forces Medical Services.
  • To provide training to under-graduate and post-graduate medical and nursing students with assured career prospects in the defence services.
  • To provide high quality medical care to the Armed Forces personnel and their families.

    • Functions of AFMC

  • Imparting medical education to undergraduate and postgraduate students.
  • Providing training to nursing and paramedical staff.
  • Conducting research in various fields of medicine.
  • Providing tertiary healthcare to Armed Forces personnel and their dependents.
  • Acting as a referral center for the Armed Forces hospitals in the region.
  • Providing medical support during disasters and national emergencies.

🎓 Courses Offered

AFMC offers various undergraduate and postgraduate courses in medical sciences. The college is known for its MBBS program, which is highly competitive. The curriculum is designed to provide comprehensive medical education along with military training to prepare students for service in the Armed Forces Medical Services.

UNDERGRADUATE EDUCATION

The undergraduate program at AFMC is the MBBS course, which is of 4.5 years duration followed by a compulsory internship of 1 year. The course is affiliated to the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Nashik and recognized by the Medical Council of India. The admission to the MBBS course is through the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) followed by an interview and medical examination.

Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) :

The MBBS course at AFMC is designed to provide comprehensive medical education along with military training. The course structure includes:

Phase Duration (years) Training
First One-and-a-half Pre-Clinical & Para-Clinical
Second One Para-clinical
Third Two Clinical
Internship One Compulsory rotation in various departments

At present, 150 students (115 boys and 35 girls) are admitted every year to the MBBS course. The selection is based on NEET score, followed by a screening process including psychological assessment test, interview, and medical examination. All selected students are required to execute a bond to serve in the Armed Forces Medical Services for a minimum period of seven years after completion of the course.

Nursing Education:

AFMC also offers a B.Sc Nursing program which is of 4 years duration. The program is affiliated to the Maharashtra University of Health Sciences, Nashik and recognized by the Indian Nursing Council. The admission to the B.Sc Nursing program is also through a competitive entrance examination followed by an interview and medical examination.

🏕️ Campus Life

The AFMC campus is spread over approximately 150 acres in Pune, Maharashtra. The campus is well-equipped with modern facilities for academics, sports, and recreation. The college has separate hostels for boys and girls with all necessary amenities. The campus also houses a Command Hospital, which serves as a teaching hospital for the students.

Hostel Life

AFMC is a fully residential college with separate hostels for boys and girls. All undergraduate students are provided accommodation in the hostels. The hostels are well-furnished with all necessary amenities including Wi-Fi, common rooms, and recreational facilities. The mess provides nutritious and hygienic food to the students. The hostel life at AFMC is known for its discipline and camaraderie among students.

Sports and Extracurricular Activities

AFMC has excellent sports facilities including grounds for cricket, football, hockey, and athletics. The college also has facilities for indoor games like table tennis, badminton, and basketball. The college encourages students to participate in various extracurricular activities including cultural events, debates, and quizzes. The annual college festival "Silhouette" is a much-awaited event that showcases the talents of AFMC students.

Military Training

As part of the curriculum, AFMC students undergo military training to prepare them for service in the Armed Forces. This includes physical training, drill, and basic military skills. The students are also exposed to the military way of life and discipline. The military training at AFMC is designed to instill leadership qualities and a sense of duty towards the nation.

Healthcare Facilities

AFMC has a 1500-bed Command Hospital attached to it, which provides tertiary healthcare services to Armed Forces personnel and their families. The hospital is equipped with state-of-the-art medical equipment and facilities. The students get hands-on clinical training at this hospital. The college also has a well-equipped medical center for the health check-up and treatment of students.

Career Opportunities After AFMC

AFMC graduates are commissioned as officers in the Armed Forces Medical Services. They serve in the Army, Navy, or Air Force medical corps. After completing the mandatory service period, they have various career opportunities in both government and private sectors.

Career Path Details
Armed Forces Medical Services Commissioned as officers with rank of Captain/Equivalent
Postgraduate Studies Opportunities for MD/MS/DM/MCh in Armed Forces hospitals
Research Opportunities Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO)
Teaching Positions Faculty positions in AFMC and other medical colleges
Private Practice After completing mandatory service period
Corporate Healthcare Hospitals, healthcare companies, insurance sector
