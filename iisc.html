Basic Information

The Indian Institute of Science (IISc) is a public, deemed, research university for higher education and research in science, engineering, design, and management. It is located in Bangalore, in the Indian state of Karnataka. Established in 1909 with active support from Jamsetji Tata and H.H. Sir Krishnaraja Wodeyar IV, the Maharaja of Mysore, it is also locally known as the "Tata Institute". It has been ranked first among Indian universities in the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) university rankings for seven consecutive years since 2016.

History

After an accident in 1896, Jamsetji Tata distributed his wealth for public welfare. Inspired by Western scientists and discussions with Swami Vivekananda, he decided to establish a research institution in India. With support from the Maharaja of Mysore and the British government, the Indian Institute of Science was founded in 1909. Initially focused on advanced research and higher education in sciences, it has grown into a premier institution offering programs in engineering, design, and management. IISc has played a crucial role in India's scientific development, contributing to advancements in various fields and producing numerous renowned scientists and researchers.

Vision and Mission

  • To be among the world's foremost academic institutions through pursuit of excellence in research and promotion of innovation by offering world-class education to train future leaders in science and technology and by applying science and technology breakthroughs for India's wealth creation and social welfare.
  • To provide advanced scientific and technological research and education to attract and develop the best talent.
  • To create an environment that encourages collaborative research and innovation.

Objectives

  • To advance knowledge and educate students in science, technology, and other areas of scholarship that will best serve the nation and the world in the 21st century.
  • To generate, disseminate, and preserve knowledge, and to work with others to bring this knowledge to bear on the world's great challenges.
  • To provide a platform for interdisciplinary research and education.
  • To contribute to the development of India through scientific and technological research and innovation.

üèï Campus Life

The Indian Institute of Science (IISc) campus is spread over 400 acres of lush greenery in the heart of Bangalore. The campus provides a serene and intellectually stimulating environment for students, faculty, and researchers. With its blend of historic buildings and modern facilities, IISc offers a unique academic experience.

Hostels and Accommodation

IISc provides on-campus accommodation to most of its students. The institute has several hostels for undergraduate, postgraduate, and research students. Each hostel has its own mess, common room, and recreational facilities. The hostels are equipped with Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and 24/7 security. The hostel life at IISc is vibrant, with students from diverse backgrounds and cultures living together, fostering a sense of community and collaboration.

Sports and Recreation

IISc encourages students to maintain a healthy balance between academics and physical activities. The campus has excellent sports facilities, including a gymnasium, swimming pool, tennis courts, basketball courts, cricket ground, and football ground. The institute also has a student activity center where various cultural and recreational activities are organized throughout the year.

Cultural Activities

Cultural life at IISc is vibrant and diverse. The institute has several student clubs and societies that organize various cultural events throughout the year. The annual cultural festival, "Pravega," is one of the most anticipated events on campus, featuring music, dance, drama, art, and literature. The festival attracts participants from colleges across the country and provides a platform for students to showcase their talents.

Facilities and Amenities

IISc provides state-of-the-art facilities to support academic and research activities. The campus has well-equipped laboratories, advanced research centers, a central library with a vast collection of books and journals, and modern classrooms. The institute also has a health center, banking facilities, post office, and several cafeterias and food courts that cater to the diverse culinary preferences of the campus community.
