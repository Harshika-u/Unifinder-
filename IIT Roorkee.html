Basic Information

The Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee (abbreviated IIT Roorkee) is a public technical and research university located in Roorkee, Uttarakhand, India, headed by Director Prof. K. K. Pant. It was formerly known as University of Roorkee (1948-2001) and Thomason College of Civil Engineering (1847-1948). Established in 1847 in British India by the then lieutenant governor of the North Western Provinces, James Thomason, it is the oldest technical institution in Asia. It was given university status in 1949 and was converted into an Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) in 2001, thus becoming the seventh IIT to be declared.

History

The institute was established in 1847 by James Thomason, the lieutenant governor of the North Western Provinces, during the British colonial period. It was initially named Thomason College of Civil Engineering and was established to train personnel for the construction of the Ganges Canal. The college was renamed as Thomason College of Civil Engineering in 1854. It became the first technical institution in India to be granted university status in 1949, and was renamed as the University of Roorkee. In 2001, the university was converted into an Indian Institute of Technology, making it the seventh IIT in the country.

üèïÔ∏è Campus Life

IIT Roorkee's campus life is a perfect blend of academics, extracurricular activities, and cultural events. Spread over 365 acres, the campus provides a vibrant environment for students to grow and excel in various fields. The institute has state-of-the-art facilities including modern classrooms, well-equipped laboratories, a central library, sports complexes, and comfortable hostels.

Hostel Life

IIT Roorkee has 12 hostels for boys and 3 hostels for girls, each with its own unique culture and traditions. The hostels are equipped with modern amenities including Wi-Fi, mess facilities, common rooms, and recreational areas. Each hostel has its own council that organizes various intra-hostel events and activities. The hostel life at IIT Roorkee fosters a sense of community and lifelong friendships among students.

Student Clubs and Societies

IIT Roorkee has a vibrant student community with over 50 clubs and societies covering various interests. These include technical clubs like Robotics Club, Programming Club, and Electronics Club; cultural clubs like Music Club, Dance Club, and Dramatics Club; sports clubs for various games and athletic activities; and social service clubs like NSS and Prayas. These clubs provide students with opportunities to explore their interests, develop new skills, and interact with like-minded individuals.

Festivals and Events

IIT Roorkee hosts several major festivals and events throughout the year. The two most prominent ones are:

Thomso: The annual cultural festival of IIT Roorkee, usually held in October. It is one of the largest college festivals in India, attracting participants from colleges across the country. The festival features music concerts, dance competitions, dramatics, fashion shows, art exhibitions, and various other cultural events.

Cognizance: The annual technical festival of IIT Roorkee, usually held in March. It is one of the largest technical festivals in Asia, featuring workshops, lectures by eminent personalities, exhibitions, competitions, and technical displays. The festival provides a platform for students to showcase their technical skills and knowledge.

Sports and Recreation

IIT Roorkee has excellent sports facilities including a stadium, swimming pool, gymnasium, tennis courts, basketball courts, volleyball courts, and grounds for cricket, football, and hockey. The institute encourages sports and physical activities among students. The inter-hostel and inter-college tournaments are organized regularly, providing students with opportunities to showcase their sporting talents. The institute also has a dedicated Sports Council that manages and promotes sports activities on campus.
