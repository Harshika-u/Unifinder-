History

The history of JIPMER dates back to 1823 when it was established as "Ã‰cole de MÃ©dicine de PondichÃ©ry" by the French colonial government in Puducherry. It was one of the earliest institutions to teach Western medicine in India. After India gained independence, the institution was renamed Dhanvantri Medical College.

In 1956, the institution was upgraded to a regional postgraduate center and renamed Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education & Research (JIPMER) in honor of India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. This upgrade marked the beginning of its journey as a center for postgraduate medical education and research.

A significant milestone in JIPMER's history came in 2008 when the Indian Parliament passed the JIPMER Act, declaring it an Institute of National Importance. This status granted JIPMER greater autonomy in academic, administrative, and financial matters. Over the years, JIPMER has grown into a premier medical institution, known for its excellence in medical education, research, and patient care.

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS

JIPMER offers several undergraduate programs in medicine, nursing, and allied health sciences. The flagship program is the MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery), which has an annual intake of 200 students across its two campuses (150 in Puducherry and 50 in Karaikal). The college also offers BSc programs in Nursing, Medical Laboratory Technology, and various other allied health disciplines.

Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) :

The MBBS program at JIPMER is a 5.5-year course including a one-year compulsory rotating internship. The curriculum is designed to provide a strong foundation in medical sciences and extensive clinical exposure. Admission is based on the NEET UG score followed by counseling conducted by JIPMER.

Admission to the MBBS program is highly competitive, with 200 seats available annually (150 in Puducherry and 50 in Karaikal). The selection process is based on the NEET UG score followed by counseling conducted by JIPMER. The college follows a reservation policy as per Government of India norms.
