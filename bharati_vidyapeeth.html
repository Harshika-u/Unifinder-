Basic Information

Bharati Vidyapeeth Deemed University is a premier higher education institution in India, established in 1964 by Dr. Patangrao Kadam. The institution was granted the status of "Deemed to be University" in 1996 by the Government of India on the recommendation of the University Grants Commission. The university has been accredited with an 'A+' grade by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) with a CGPA of 3.52, which is a testament to its academic excellence and infrastructure.

The university operates from its main campus in Pune, Maharashtra, and has expanded its presence across India with campuses in Delhi NCR, Mumbai, Sangli, Kolhapur, Solapur, Karad, and other locations. Bharati Vidyapeeth is known for its commitment to providing quality education in diverse fields including medicine, dentistry, engineering, management, law, social sciences, and arts.

History

Bharati Vidyapeeth had a very humble beginning. It was established in 1964 by Dr. Patangrao Kadam, who brought into reality a dream that was nurtured since his childhood. The organization began with a small school and a college in Pune, Maharashtra.

The journey of Bharati Vidyapeeth from a small educational institution to a multi-disciplinary, multi-campus university is a story of vision, determination, and commitment. Dr. Kadam's vision was to bring about social transformation through dynamic education, and this vision has been the guiding principle of the institution since its inception.

In 1996, the Government of India, on the recommendation of the University Grants Commission, granted the status of "Deemed to be University" to a cluster of 12 institutions of Bharati Vidyapeeth in recognition of their academic excellence. This was a significant milestone in the history of the institution.

Over the years, Bharati Vidyapeeth has grown exponentially, establishing numerous schools, colleges, and research centers across India. Today, it stands as one of the largest multi-disciplinary, multi-campus universities in the country, with a student population of over 100,000.
