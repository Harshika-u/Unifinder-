Basic Information

Kasturba Medical College (KMC), Manipal is a premier medical college in India, established in 1953 by Dr. T.M.A. Pai. It is part of the Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE), a deemed to be university and Institute of Eminence. KMC Manipal is one of the first private medical colleges in India and has been consistently ranked among the top medical colleges in the country by NIRF.

The college is located in the university town of Manipal, Karnataka, and is known for its academic excellence, state-of-the-art infrastructure, and research facilities. KMC Manipal has two teaching hospitals - Kasturba Hospital and Dr. T.M.A. Pai Hospital, which provide extensive clinical exposure to students. The institution has collaborations with several international universities and research organizations, facilitating global exposure and research opportunities for students and faculty.

History

Kasturba Medical College, Manipal was established in 1953 by Dr. T.M.A. Pai, a visionary educationist and philanthropist. It was the first self-financing medical college in India and was started with the objective of providing quality medical education to students from all sections of society.

Initially, the college was affiliated to Karnataka University and later to Mysore University. In 1993, when the Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE) was granted deemed to be university status, KMC became a constituent college of MAHE. Over the years, the college has grown in stature and has become one of the most sought-after medical institutions in India.

KMC Manipal has been at the forefront of medical education and research in the country. It has introduced several innovative teaching methodologies and has been a pioneer in establishing simulation-based medical education in India. The college has also been actively involved in community health programs and has made significant contributions to healthcare delivery in the region.

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS

KMC Manipal offers several undergraduate programs in medicine, nursing, and allied health sciences. The flagship program is the MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery), which has an annual intake of 250 students. The college also offers BSc programs in Nursing, Medical Laboratory Technology, Radiography, and various other allied health disciplines.

Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) :

The MBBS program at KMC Manipal is a 5.5-year course including a one-year compulsory rotating internship. The curriculum is designed to provide a strong foundation in medical sciences and extensive clinical exposure. Admission is based on the NEET UG score followed by counseling conducted by DGHS.

Second One-and-a-half Para-clinical (Pathology, Microbiology, Pharmacology, etc.)

Internship One Compulsory rotation in various departments

Admission to the MBBS program is highly competitive, with 250 seats available annually. The selection process is based on the NEET UG score followed by counseling conducted by DGHS. The college follows a reservation policy as per Government of India norms.
