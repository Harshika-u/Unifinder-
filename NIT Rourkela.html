Basic Information

The National Institute of Technology Calicut (NIT-Calicut or NIT-C) is a public technical university and an institute of national importance governed by the NIT Act passed by the Parliament of India. The campus is situated 22 kilometres (14 mi) northeast of Kozhikode, on the Kozhikode‚ÄìMukkam Road. It was established in 1961 and was known as Calicut Regional Engineering College (CREC) until 2002. It is one of the National Institutes of Technology campuses established by the Government of India to impart high standard technical education to students from all over the country. NIT Calicut hosts a supercomputer on its campus, and has a dedicated nanotechnology department. NIT Calicut is ranked as one of the prestigious engineering institutions in India.

History

NNIT Rourkela began life as Regional Engineering College (REC), Rourkela, inaugurated on Independence Day, 1961, emphasizing India‚Äôs drive for technical self-sufficiency. The institution was granted full autonomy and rechristened as NIT Rourkela in 2002, gaining the prestigious status of Institute of National Importance by the NIT Act. The school has expanded from humble beginnings‚Äîone building housing everything‚Äîto a 700-acre campus supporting over 7,700 students, 390+ faculty, and a robust research culture. Recognized as a premier institute for science, technology, and research, NITR continues to punch above its weight in national and international rankings.
