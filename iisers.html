About IISERs

The Indian Institutes of Science Education and Research (IISERs) are a group of premier autonomous science education and research institutes in India. Established by the Government of India through the Ministry of Education (formerly Ministry of Human Resource Development), these institutions are dedicated to science education and research in an integrated manner. The first IISER was established in Pune in 2006, followed by Mohali and Kolkata in 2007, Bhopal and Thiruvananthapuram in 2008, Tirupati in 2015, and Berhampur in 2016. IISERs are designed to integrate undergraduate education with postgraduate and doctoral research, creating a unique academic environment that fosters scientific inquiry and innovation.

History

The concept of IISERs was proposed by the Scientific Advisory Council to the Prime Minister of India in 2005. The vision was to create institutions that would integrate undergraduate education with postgraduate and doctoral research, similar to the model of the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) in Bangalore. The first IISER was established in Pune in 2006, followed by Mohali and Kolkata in 2007. Bhopal and Thiruvananthapuram were established in 2008, Tirupati in 2015, and Berhampur in 2016. These institutions were created to address the need for quality science education and research in India and to nurture scientific talent from an early stage. Over the years, IISERs have grown into centers of excellence in science education and research, contributing significantly to India's scientific development.

Vision and Mission

  • To create institutions of scientific excellence dedicated to teaching and research in an integrated manner.
  • To nurture scientific curiosity and creativity among students through innovative teaching methods and research opportunities.
  • To foster a culture of interdisciplinary research and education.

Objectives

  • To provide high-quality science education integrated with cutting-edge research.
  • To attract and nurture young minds towards scientific research.
  • To create a unique academic environment that integrates undergraduate and postgraduate education with research.
  • To contribute to the development of India through scientific research and innovation.

üèï Campus Life

The IISER campuses are spread across 100-200 acres of lush greenery in different parts of India. Each campus provides a serene and intellectually stimulating environment for students, faculty, and researchers. With their blend of modern architecture and natural surroundings, IISERs offer a unique academic experience.

Hostels and Accommodation

IISERs provide on-campus accommodation to most of their students. Each institute has several hostels for undergraduate, postgraduate, and research students. Each hostel has its own mess, common room, and recreational facilities. The hostels are equipped with Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and 24/7 security. The hostel life at IISERs is vibrant, with students from diverse backgrounds and cultures living together, fostering a sense of community and collaboration.

Sports and Recreation

IISERs encourage students to maintain a healthy balance between academics and physical activities. The campuses have excellent sports facilities, including gymnasiums, swimming pools, tennis courts, basketball courts, cricket grounds, and football grounds. The institutes also have student activity centers where various cultural and recreational activities are organized throughout the year.

Cultural Activities

Cultural life at IISERs is vibrant and diverse. The institutes have several student clubs and societies that organize various cultural events throughout the year. Annual cultural festivals like "Inquire" (IISER Pune), "Pragyan" (IISER Mohali), and "Equinox" (IISER Kolkata) are among the most anticipated events on campus, featuring music, dance, drama, art, and literature. These festivals attract participants from colleges across the country and provide a platform for students to showcase their talents.

Facilities and Amenities

IISERs provide state-of-the-art facilities to support academic and research activities. The campuses have well-equipped laboratories, advanced research centers, central libraries with vast collections of books and journals, and modern classrooms. The institutes also have health centers, banking facilities, post offices, and several cafeterias and food courts that cater to the diverse culinary preferences of the campus community.
