Basic Information

The University of Hyderabad is a premier institution of postgraduate teaching and research in the country. It was established by an Act of Parliament in 1974 as a Central University. The university has been recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) as a 'University with Potential for Excellence' and has been awarded the status of 'Institution of Eminence' (IoE) by the Government of India. It is also accredited with the highest A++ grade by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC).

History

The University of Hyderabad was established in 1974 by an Act of Parliament. It was founded with the vision of providing quality higher education and promoting research in various disciplines. The university started functioning from 1974-75 with Prof. Gurbaksh Singh as its first Vice-Chancellor. Initially, the university operated from a temporary campus, and the foundation stone for the permanent campus was laid in 1976. The university gradually shifted to its present campus in Gachibowli between 1978 and 1985.

Over the years, the university has grown into a major center of higher learning and research in India. It has established itself as a leading institution in the country, known for its academic excellence, research output, and innovation. The university has consistently been ranked among the top universities in India by various national and international ranking agencies.

Objectives of the University

  • To disseminate and advance knowledge by providing instructional and research facilities in such branches of learning as it may deem fit.
  • To make provisions for integrated courses in humanities, science and technology in the educational programmes of the University.
  • To take appropriate measures for promoting inter-disciplinary studies and research.
  • To educate and train manpower for the development of the country.
  • To establish linkages with industries for the promotion of science and technology.
  • To pay special attention to the improvement of the social and economic conditions and welfare of the people of India.

🏕️ Campus Life

The University of Hyderabad campus is spread over 2300 acres of lush green landscape, providing an ideal environment for learning and research. The campus is known for its scenic beauty, with rocks, boulders, and natural vegetation, making it one of the most beautiful campuses in India. The university provides a vibrant and inclusive campus life, with various facilities and activities to cater to the diverse interests of its students.

Hostel Life

The university provides hostel accommodation to a majority of its students. There are 22 hostels on campus, including separate hostels for men and women. Each hostel has its own mess, common room, and recreational facilities. The hostels are equipped with Wi-Fi, solar heating systems, and 24-hour security. The hostel life at the university is vibrant, with various cultural and sports activities organized throughout the year.

The university also provides accommodation to international students in a separate International Hostel. The hostel fees are subsidized and vary depending on the type of accommodation and facilities provided.

Sports and Recreation

The university provides excellent sports and recreational facilities to its students. There are playgrounds for cricket, football, hockey, volleyball, and basketball. The university also has a well-equipped gymnasium, tennis courts, and a swimming pool. The Sports Board of the university organizes various sports events and tournaments throughout the year.

The university also has a Students' Union, which represents the student community and organizes various cultural, literary, and sports activities. The Students' Union also plays an active role in addressing the concerns and issues of the students.

Cultural Activities

The university has a rich cultural life, with various cultural events and festivals organized throughout the year. The annual cultural festival of the university, known as "Sukoon", is one of the most popular events on campus. The festival includes various cultural performances, competitions, and workshops, providing a platform for students to showcase their talents.

The university also has various student clubs and societies, such as the Dramatics Club, Music Club, Dance Club, Photography Club, and Literary Club, which organize various activities and events throughout the year.

Other Facilities

The university provides various other facilities to its students, including a well-equipped library with a vast collection of books, journals, and e-resources; a health center with qualified medical professionals; banking and postal services; and a shopping complex with various shops and eateries. The campus also has a guest house for visiting faculty and guests.

The university is well-connected to the city by public transport, with buses and autos available from the campus to various parts of the city. The university also provides shuttle services within the campus for the convenience of students and staff.

Placement Process

The placement process at the University of Hyderabad is well-structured and transparent. The Placement and Career Guidance Cell coordinates with various companies and organizations to facilitate campus recruitment. The placement process typically includes the following steps:

  1. Pre-placement talks by companies
  2. Registration of students for placement
  3. Written tests (aptitude, technical, etc.)
  4. Group discussions
  5. Technical interviews
  6. HR interviews
  7. Offer letters and joining formalities

The university also provides training and guidance to students to enhance their employability. This includes workshops on resume writing, interview skills, group discussions, and technical skills. The university also organizes mock interviews and aptitude tests to prepare students for the placement process.
