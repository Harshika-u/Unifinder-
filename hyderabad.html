Basic Information

The University of Hyderabad is a premier central university located in Hyderabad, Telangana. Established in 1974 by an Act of Parliament, it is one of India's major institutions of postgraduate teaching and research. The university has been consistently ranked among the top universities in India by the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF). With its sprawling campus of over 2300 acres, the university provides a serene environment for academic pursuits and is home to a diverse community of students and faculty from across the country and abroad.

History

The University of Hyderabad was established in 1974 by an Act of Parliament as a Central University. The foundation stone was laid on October 10, 1974, by the then President of India, Fakhruddin Ali Ahmed. The university started functioning in 1975 with Prof. Gurbaksh Singh as its first Vice-Chancellor. Initially, the university operated from a temporary campus, but it moved to its current permanent campus in Gachibowli in 1978. Over the years, the university has grown into a major center of teaching and research, known for its academic excellence and innovative programs.

RESEARCH PROGRAMS

The University of Hyderabad is renowned for its research programs across various disciplines. The institute offers PhD programs in all departments, providing students with the opportunity to work on cutting-edge research projects under the guidance of experienced faculty members.

PhD Programs:

The PhD program at the University of Hyderabad is designed to train students to become independent researchers who can contribute to the advancement of knowledge in their chosen fields. The program involves coursework, comprehensive examinations, and original research leading to a thesis.

üèï Campus Life

The University of Hyderabad campus is spread over 2300 acres of lush greenery in Hyderabad, Telangana. The campus provides a serene and intellectually stimulating environment for students, faculty, and researchers. With its modern infrastructure and natural beauty, the university offers a unique academic experience in one of India's major technology hubs.

Hostels and Accommodation

The University of Hyderabad provides on-campus accommodation to most of its students. The university has several hostels for undergraduate, postgraduate, and research students, separate for boys and girls. Each hostel has its own mess, common room, and recreational facilities. The hostels are equipped with Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and 24/7 security. The hostel life at the university is vibrant, with students from diverse backgrounds and cultures living together, fostering a sense of community and collaboration.

Sports and Recreation

The University of Hyderabad encourages students to maintain a healthy balance between academics and physical activities. The campus has excellent sports facilities, including a stadium, gymnasium, swimming pool, tennis courts, basketball courts, cricket ground, and football ground. The university also has a student activity center where various cultural and recreational activities are organized throughout the year.

Cultural Activities

Cultural life at the University of Hyderabad is vibrant and diverse. The university has several student clubs and societies that organize various cultural events throughout the year. The annual cultural festival, "Sukoon," is one of the most anticipated events on campus, featuring music, dance, drama, art, and literature. The festival attracts participants from colleges across the country and provides a platform for students to showcase their talents.

Facilities and Amenities

The University of Hyderabad provides state-of-the-art facilities to support academic and research activities. The campus has well-equipped laboratories, advanced research centers, a central library with a vast collection of books and journals, and modern classrooms. The university also has a health center, banking facilities, post office, shopping complex, and several cafeterias that cater to the diverse culinary preferences of the campus community. The Indira Gandhi Memorial Library at the university is one of the largest university libraries in India with over 400,000 volumes.
