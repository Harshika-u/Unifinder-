Basic Information

Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU) is a public central university located in New Delhi, India. It was established in 1969 by an act of parliament. Named after Jawaharlal Nehru, the first Prime Minister of India, JNU is widely regarded as one of the premier universities in India. The university is known for its leading research and teaching faculties in social sciences, humanities, and sciences. JNU has been granted the status of 'University of Excellence' by the University Grants Commission (UGC). It has been ranked second among all universities in India by the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) in 2024.

History

Jawaharlal Nehru University was established in 1969 by an act of parliament. It was named after India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru. G. Parthasarathy was the first vice-chancellor of the university. The Bill for the establishment of Jawaharlal Nehru University was placed in the Rajya Sabha on September 1, 1965, by the then Education Minister, M.C. Chagla. The JNU Bill was passed in Lok Sabha on 16th November 1966 and the JNU Act came into force on 22nd December 1966.

The objective of the university was to promote the study of national integration, social justice, secularism, democratic way of life, international understanding and scientific approach in solving the problems of society. Over the years, JNU has grown into a large university with several centers, schools, and special centers.

JNU has been at the forefront of academic excellence and social consciousness. It has been a breeding ground for various social and political movements in India. The university has been known for its strong left-leaning student politics and has produced several prominent politicians, academics, and activists.

Vision and Mission

  • To promote national integration, social justice, democratic way of life, secularism, and international understanding.
  • To create a community of scholars and students committed to the pursuit of knowledge and innovation.
  • To develop a critical understanding of society and its institutions.
  • To foster interdisciplinary research and teaching.
  • To provide access to higher education for students from all sections of society.
  • To contribute to the development of a just and equitable society through research, teaching, and extension activities.

🏕️ Campus Life

Jawaharlal Nehru University is not just an academic institution but a vibrant community that fosters intellectual growth, cultural diversity, and social consciousness. Spread over a sprawling 1019 acres, the JNU campus is a microcosm of India's diversity, with students from all corners of the country and different parts of the world. The campus is known for its lush greenery, with the Aravalli range forming a picturesque backdrop.

Hostel Life

JNU provides residential facilities to its students through its 18 hostels, including one for married students. These hostels are not just places to stay but centers of intellectual and cultural activities. Each hostel has a common room, reading room, TV room, and sports facilities. The hostels have their own messes that provide affordable and nutritious food. The hostel life at JNU is known for its democratic ethos, with hostel committees elected by the residents managing various aspects of hostel life.

Cultural and Intellectual Activities

JNU is known for its vibrant cultural and intellectual life. The university hosts numerous seminars, conferences, and workshops throughout the year, featuring eminent scholars, artists, and public figures from India and abroad. The JNU Students' Union (JNUSU) is one of the most active student bodies in the country, organizing various cultural, social, and political events.

The annual cultural festival of JNU, called "North East Festival," celebrates the cultural diversity of India's northeastern states. Other popular events include the International Film Festival, the Literary Festival, and various departmental festivals that showcase the academic and cultural talents of students.

Sports and Recreation

JNU provides excellent sports and recreational facilities to its students. The university has a sports complex with facilities for various indoor and outdoor games, including cricket, football, hockey, volleyball, basketball, tennis, badminton, and table tennis. The university also has a gymnasium and a swimming pool. The Annual Sports Meet is a major event that sees enthusiastic participation from students across all hostels and departments.

Political Activism

JNU is known for its vibrant political culture and student activism. The university has been a breeding ground for various social and political movements in India. The campus is known for its democratic ethos, with students actively participating in debates and discussions on various social, political, and economic issues. The JNU Students' Union elections are held annually through direct voting and are known for their high voter turnout and intense political campaigning.

JNU Placements

Jawaharlal Nehru University has a dedicated Placement Cell that facilitates job opportunities for its students. While JNU is primarily known for its academic excellence and research output, the Placement Cell works to connect students with potential employers in various sectors.

The Placement Cell organizes campus recruitment drives, workshops, and training programs to enhance the employability of students. It also provides guidance and counseling to students regarding career options and higher education opportunities.

JNU graduates are highly sought after in government sectors, research organizations, NGOs, international organizations, media houses, and multinational companies. The university's strong emphasis on critical thinking, research skills, and social consciousness makes its graduates valuable assets in various fields.
