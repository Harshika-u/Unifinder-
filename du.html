Basic Information

The University of Delhi, informally known as Delhi University (DU), is a collegiate public central university located in New Delhi, India. It was founded in 1922 by an Act of the Central Legislative Assembly and is recognized as an Institute of Eminence by the University Grants Commission (UGC). The university has 16 faculties and 86 departments distributed across its North and South campuses. It is one of the largest institutions of higher learning in India with over 132,000 students.

History

The University of Delhi was established in 1922 as a unitary, teaching and residential university by an Act of the then Central Legislative Assembly of British India. The university initially had three colleges: St. Stephen's College, Hindu College, and Ramjas College. The university has since grown significantly, establishing numerous colleges and departments. In 1973, the university was divided into the North and South campuses to accommodate its expansion. Over the years, Delhi University has produced numerous eminent scholars, scientists, political leaders, and artists who have made significant contributions to their respective fields and to the nation.

RESEARCH PROGRAMS

The University of Delhi is renowned for its research programs across various disciplines. The university offers PhD programs in all departments, providing students with the opportunity to work on cutting-edge research projects under the guidance of experienced faculty members.

PhD Programs:

The PhD program at Delhi University is designed to train students to become independent researchers who can contribute to the advancement of knowledge in their chosen fields. The program involves coursework, comprehensive examinations, and original research leading to a thesis.

Research Areas:

  • Physics: Condensed Matter Physics, Nuclear Physics, Electronics, Astrophysics
  • Chemistry: Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, Physical Chemistry, Analytical Chemistry
  • Mathematics: Pure Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Statistics
  • Botany: Plant Physiology, Genetics, Plant Pathology, Environmental Botany
  • Zoology: Cell Biology, Genetics, Endocrinology, Fisheries
  • Geology: Petrology, Mineralogy, Paleontology, Hydrogeology
  • Computer Science: Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Computer Networks, Software Engineering

Admission to the PhD program is based on the Delhi University Entrance Test (DUET) followed by interviews. The university provides fellowships and research assistantships to support PhD students during their studies.

üèï Campus Life

The University of Delhi has two main campuses - North Campus and South Campus. The North Campus is spread over 150 acres and houses the university's administrative offices, central library, and several departments. The South Campus is spread over 69 acres and focuses on sciences and interdisciplinary studies. The campuses provide a vibrant and intellectually stimulating environment for students, faculty, and researchers.

Hostels and Accommodation

The University of Delhi provides on-campus accommodation to a limited number of students. The university has several hostels for undergraduate, postgraduate, and research students, separate for boys and girls. Each hostel has its own mess, common room, and recreational facilities. The hostels are equipped with Wi-Fi, laundry facilities, and 24/7 security. Due to limited seats, most students prefer to stay in PG accommodations or rented flats near the campus.

Sports and Recreation

The University of Delhi encourages students to maintain a healthy balance between academics and physical activities. The campus has excellent sports facilities, including a stadium, gymnasium, swimming pool, tennis courts, basketball courts, cricket ground, and football ground. The university also has a student activity center where various cultural and recreational activities are organized throughout the year.

Cultural Activities

Cultural life at Delhi University is vibrant and diverse. The university has several student clubs and societies that organize various cultural events throughout the year. The annual cultural festival, "Antardhvani," is one of the most anticipated events on campus, featuring music, dance, drama, art, and literature. The festival attracts participants from colleges across the country and provides a platform for students to showcase their talents.

Facilities and Amenities

The University of Delhi provides state-of-the-art facilities to support academic and research activities. The campus has well-equipped laboratories, advanced research centers, a central library with a vast collection of books and journals, and modern classrooms. The university also has a health center, banking facilities, post office, shopping complex, and several cafeterias that cater to the diverse culinary preferences of the campus community. The central library at Delhi University is one of the largest university libraries in India with over 1.5 million volumes.
